\begin{abstract}
Solving harder trigonometric equations 
\end{abstract}

Sometimes the equations are easier if you square both sides (use Pythagorean Identities) but you must check your answers then.

\subsection{Examples}
\begin{enumerate}[(a)]
\item $\cos(\theta) + 1 = \sin(\theta)$ where $0 \leq \theta < 2\pi$
\item Find where $f(x) = \sin(x)$ and $g(x) = \cos(x)$ intersect.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Equations with Trig Functions of Multiples of Angles}

Solve it like normal and then at the very end get rid of the coefficient.

\subsection{Examples}
\begin{enumerate}[(a)]
\item $2\sin(3\theta) -1 = 0$ on $[2\pi,4\pi)$
\item $\sqrt[]{3}\tan(\frac{\theta}{2}) -1 = 0$ on $[0,4\pi)$
\end{enumerate}
